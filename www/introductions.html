Introductions

Hi, my name is Jon Stevens. I'm the primary leader for the Scarab project. I currently work on many different OS projects: Java Apache, Jakarta and Working-Dogs.com. I'm pretty much involved in one way or another with all of the sub projects within these projects, including Turbine, Apache JServ, ECS, Village, Town, Jyve, etc..

A few years ago I saw the complete lack of a decent bug tracking system as something that would be extremely popular on the web. Thus, I have been working for the last couple years building these projects up to become the infrastructure for this very project. It gives me great pleasure to finally be able to work on my end goal. ;-)

I also work for Collab.Net who is sponsoring my time to work on this project during work hours. Collab.Net is doing some of the most interesting stuff in OS these days...essentially tying a valid and solid business model around Open Source development.This is being done through our SourceXchange and Tigris.org websites. I have to give the "we need talented people" plug because I want to help find more talented people to work here with us on these cool projects...

-jon


 

Hi, all. My desire to find/create a more flexible alternative to Bugzilla led me to this project. While I am by no means an expert on the subject, Bugzilla seems to have the conceptual issues of bug-tracking defined quite well (e.g., the lifecycle of a bug). However, it's not written in my language of choice and is not IMO amenable to large-scale modifications.

I am currently leading the development of a Turbine-based application at my company, and I need Scarab ASAP. Therefore, spending company time on Scarab development is no problem. While I intend to leave the issues of code design to those here who have far more experience in this domain, I am more than willing to help with the implementation of said design and any other coding required to get this project rolling.
